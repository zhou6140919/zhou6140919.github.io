About Me

I am 2nd year MSCS student at Virginia Tech, advised by Dr. Lifu Huang. My research interest mainly focus on Natural Language Processing, Large Language Models, and Multimodal Learning. My research focuses on advancing natural language processing and multimodal AI systems, with particular emphasis on information extraction, retrieval-augmented generation, and multimodal understanding.

Research Interests

  • Multimodal AI Systems: Pioneering work in applying vision-language models to practical applications, such as automated bug detection in video games.
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG): Creating more efficient approaches to knowledge retrieval and question answering, particularly for complex multi-hop scenarios and legal AI Applications.
  • Information Extraction and Generation: Developing novel frameworks for event extraction, with a focus on improving data efficiency through techniques like dual learning and denoised augmentation.
